The Eagle

Alfred Lord Tennyson

He clasps the craig with crooked hands;

Close to the sunin lonely hands;

Ringed with the azured world, he stands.


The Wrinkled sea beneath him crawls

He watched from his mountain walls

And like thunderbolt, he falls.
